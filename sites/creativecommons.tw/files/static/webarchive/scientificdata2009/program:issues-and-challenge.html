Role of the Public Domain and Commons Licensing in Supporting Scientific Research and Technological Innovation

公共領域與公眾授權在支持科學研究與技術發展所扮演的角色

Harlan J. Onsrud
　
Department of Spatial Information Science and Engineering
University of Maine

Professor Harlan Onsrud's research focuses on the analysis of legal, ethical, and institutional issues affecting the creation and use of digital databases and the assessment of the social impacts of spatial technologies. He teaches courses in Information Systems Law, Information Ethics, Cadastral and Land Information Systems, and Research Ethics. He is past president and current acting Executive Director of the Global Spatial Data Infrastructure Association (GSDI), past-president of the University Consortium for Geographic Information Science (UCGIS), and past Chair of the U.S. National Committee (USNC) on Data for Science and Technology (CODATA) of the National Research Council.


abstract / 摘要
  • English
  • 中文

This presentation reviews experiences from western nations that support the proposition that open access to government data is good for government and for the economic well-being and scientific advancement of nations. It argues as well that open access to research data and scholarly publications is beneficial for scientists and universities. Problems in implementing open access approaches in support of scientific data access are reviewed, a few solutions are explored, and some research challenges are itemized.

此次演講將針對西方國家中，主張政府資料的開放近用是有助於政府治理、經濟福祉、以及與國家科學發展的這種立場與經驗，作一回顧。同時也將提出，研究資料與學術出版的開放近用，對科學家以及大學都有助益的論述觀點。此次討論的議題將包括：重新檢視支持科學資料開放取用在執行方式所面臨的問題、探討解決方法、以及研究層面上的挑戰。

Model of Sharing on e-Science

e 化科學的分享模式

Simon C. Lin / 林誠謙
Eric Yen / 嚴漢偉
　
Grid & Scientific Computing Center
網格與科學計算專題中心
Academia Sinica
中央研究院

Simon C. Lin is currently responsible for the ASGC (Academia Sinica Grid Computing) which is the only WLCG Tier1 Center in Asia, and also the Asia Federation of Enabling Grid for E-sciencE (EGEE) project. In addition, he is coordinating 11 institutes from 8 Asia countries in the EUAsiaGrid project which is newly approved by EC in early 2008. Apart from the Grid activities, he is also the Project Director of International Collaboration Division for the Taiwan e-Learning and Digital Archives Program (TELDAP).

Eric Yen is a senior engineer of ASGC, and is responsible for conducting e-Science infrastructure, core technology and Grid service operation in ASGC, and to support broader collaboration within Taiwan and with other international e-Science projects.


abstract / 摘要
  • English
  • 中文

e-Science essentially changes the ways scientists conduct and share their work, the new research topic they could address, and the computing model they employ. Collaboration is the key concept of e-Science, and it is often carried out in a globally distributed team. Not just the problem itself is primarily data intensive and computational intensive, but also the members are distributed and autonomous. E-Science demands new strategies for research support and a new research infrastructure. Virtual organization is the core to formulate the sharing architecture of e-Science applications. In this report, sharing models of major e-Science projects are categorized into different levels of fabrics, resources, services and applications. Development of e-Infrastructure and the implementation of share model will be discussed. Experiences of ASGC for the operation of WLCG/EGEE Asia Pacific Regional Operation Center (APROC), Asia Federation and the WLCG Tier1 center will be outlined as well.

e 化科學 (e-Science) 改變了科學家日常的研究與分享模式、讓某些前所未能課題的研究成為可能、並促進嶄新的計算與分析方法。實際上，合作為 e 化科學之核心，這些研究課題本身不僅有巨量資料與巨量計算的特性，且通常是分散於全球團隊的合作。因此，e 化科學帶動了新的研究支援方法，並促成新的研究基礎架構之演進。虛擬組織 (virtual organization) 為 e 化科學應用中建立合作與支援分享架構之基礎。在本報告中，我們分類整理了 e 化科學發展至今的主要分享模式，區分為基礎設施、資源、服務與應用等不同層次。這些分享模式的實現方法也將提出討論。我國過去於全球網格亞洲中心，以及亞太地區網格維運中心的發展經驗也將一併說明。

Issues and Challenges in Public Licensing of Biodiversity Data and Publications

生物多樣性資料與出版品公眾授權的議題與挑戰

Kwang-Tsao Shao / 邵廣昭
Chau Chin Lin / 林朝欽
　
Biodiversity Research Center, Academia Sinica
中央研究院 生物多樣性研究中心
Taiwan Forest Research Institute, Council of Agriculture
行政院農業委員會 林業試驗所

Dr. Kwang-Tsao Shao is a research fellow, and the executive officer for Systematics and Biodiversity Information Division, at the Biodiversity Research Center, Academia Sinica (BRCAS). He used to be the director of the Institute of Marine Biology, National Taiwan Ocean University (1991-1994), the director of the Institute of Zoology, Academia Sinica (1996-2002), and the acting director of BRCAS (2004-2007). Fish taxonomy, marine ecology and fisheries are his major research areas. He also serves as the executive secretary of the National Committee for IUBS, DIVERSITAS, and GBIF in Taiwan.

Dr. Chau Chin Lin is a senior scientist at Taiwan Forestry Research Institute (TFRI). His research and professional interests include fire ecology, and ecoinformatics. He has over 15 years’ experience on fire ecology. Currently, he is working on developing a multi-language information management system for ILTER-EAP (International Long Term Ecological Research, East Asian-Pacific) region, and is promoting the use of ecological metadata languages to integrate and share data within the region. He received his Ph D in Fire Ecology at Clemson University, in 1999.


abstract / 摘要
  • English
  • 中文

In order to meet the tasks stipulated by the Convention on Biological Diversity, it is essential that biodiversity data be integrated and accessible; only through sufficient and shared information can the objectives of biodiversity conservation and resource sustainability be achieved. It has become a global trend to open and share research data. For example, GBIF (http://www.gbif.org) in January 2006 announced its “Recommendation on Open Access to Biodiversity Data,” IUCN is promoting the concept of Conservation Common, and the European Union has recently urged scientists to respond to the call of “public access to publicly-funded research data.”

Taiwan's government in 2005 also announced the "Government Information Disclosure Policy." However, for various reasons such as: (1) the difficulty in clarifying copyright or IPR questions, (2) the departmentalism of government agencies, (3) the negligence of government to request commissioned projects to grant it the authorization to release data, (4) the lack of giving research credit to people who provide primary data or build databases, and (5) the reluctance of researchers to release not-yet-analyzed or -published data; the amount of open accessed information and government publications on the Internet are still limited. Nevertheless, some agencies and projects have been working hard in the right direction and their experiences offer a useful reference.

The Republic of China National Committee for GBIF, at the 8th National Conference on Science and Technology as well as the Sustainable Development Research Committee of Executive Yuan, proposed that contracts with commissioned projects include a requirement of submitting raw data file in accordance with established format. Through National Science Council’s programs, TaiBIF and TaiBNET are utilized as technology and interchange platforms; additionally, there are plans to complete raw data integration by a biodiversity information management system. The Land Geographic Information System, led by Council for Economic Planning and Development, specifically included biodiversity databases and will apply service-oriented architecture to promote open data by graphic layer method. Some research institutions such as Taiwan Forestry Research Institute provide open access to their research data by way of metadatabase and research assessment system. In fact, many researchers are not against the practice of open data. As long as there are fully available tools, enhanced communication of the idea, and properly planned administrative measures, the obstacles to open data will inevitably reduce.

With respect to government publications, the commissioning party in the past when signing with an author merely specified that the original author holds the copyright and the commissioning party holds the right of use. The right for the commissioning party to authorize third party users was not spelt out, nor was the use of Creative Commons (CC) Licenses authorized. As a result, most of government publications, even if there are electronic files of them, can not be uploaded to the web for public use; a very regrettable practice. Therefore, government agencies in the future should request an author to release commissioned works under the CC Attribution License so that such publications can be made publicly available online.

生物多樣性資料的整合與公開是達成生物多樣性公約要求完成之重要工作，唯有足夠及分享的資訊，才能達成生物多樣性保育及資源永續利用的目標。研究資訊公開及分享已漸成世界潮流，例如GBIF ( http://www.gbif.org) 已在2006年1月公布其「公開生物多樣性資料之建言」; IUCN亦宣揚保育之資訊為公共財之理念(Conservation Common); 歐盟亦在最近籲請科學家連署響應將「公務預算所完成之科學研究調查資料予公開使用」。

台灣政府在2005年亦明令公布「政府資訊公開法」。但由於：(1)著作權或智財權的問題難以釐清；(2)政府機構的本位主義；(3)未要求委辦計畫之資料應授權公開；(4)提供資料或建置資料庫者沒有研究的成績；(5)多數研究人員均不願公開自己尚未分析及發表的資料等等的原因，雖使得目前已上網公開的資料及政府出版品仍然極為有限。但已有一些機構或計畫朝此方向在努力，這些經驗可以提供相當有用的參考。

GBIF中華民國委員會，在第八次全國科技會議，及在行政院永續會中提案，要求各部會在未來委辦計畫的合約書中要求執行單位需依既定之格式繳交原始資料檔。並透過國科會計畫支持TaiBIF, TaiBINET做為技術及溝通平台，另外也規劃完成生物多樣性資訊管理系統整合原始資料；經建會主導的國土地理資訊系統中特別規劃生物多樣性資料庫，以服務導向架構(service-oriented architecture)的技術促進資料以圖層的方式公開。某些研究機關如林業試驗所，透過後設資料庫及其研究考核系統公開其研究資料。事實上許多研究人員不排斥研究資料公開的作法，只要使用工具充分提供、觀念加強溝通、及行政措施適當規劃，資料公開的障礙必然減少。

除了研究的原始資料，政府出版品方面，因為過去委託單位在與作者簽約時，均只載明著作權係屬於原作者，而委託單位只有使用權，且未明載可授權給第三者使用，或是創用CC授權。故絕大多數的政府出版品即使有電子檔，亦無法上網供公眾使用，十分可惜。因此未來可以透過事先要求作者簽署可上網公開之創用CC授權。

Common-use Data System for Food Animal Genetics in Taiwan

台灣食用動物遺傳之資料共享系統

Ming-Che Wu / 吳明哲
Yung-Yu Lai / 賴永裕
Der-Yuh Lin / 林德育
Ren-Bao Liaw / 廖仁寶
　
Taiwan Livestock Research Institute, Council of Agriculture
行政院農業委員會 畜產試驗所

Ming-Che Wu, Yung-Yu Lai, Der-Yuh Lin, and Ren-Bao Liaw are with Taiwan Livestock Research Institute (Hsinhua, Tainan). Dr. Ming-Che Wu is the division chief of Breeding and Genetics at the Institute. He received his PhD degree in Animal Science at the University of Illinois, USA, in 1987.


abstract / 摘要
  • English
  • 中文

Taiwan Animal Germplasm Center (TAGC) develop and maintain the ANGRIN web management system providing a comprehensive, multiple-sources repository of all current genetic resources of farm animals for food production in Taiwan with scientific literatures and data publications. Using the ANGRIN system, TAGC and animal breeders maintain a collection of performance data of each individual animal with its pedigree, digital photos and videos available for universal free distribution on the World Wide Web. From the ANGRIN website, more than 50,000 educational print and electronic products are disseminated each year from some 3,000 farmers. Together, the streamlined publication process, universal access, and print as needed services not only reduce the cost but also expand the impact of animal breeding data on production efficiency of commercial units in animal industry sectors.

台灣畜產種原中心有管理畜產生物種原資訊網及遺傳知識庫，包括食用動物的遺傳資源現況、學術性論文、資料分析、以及出版品。應用畜產種原資訊網之網際網路屬性，台灣畜產種原中心與種畜禽場業者逐步建構個別動物之出生系譜、性能資料、數位化照片及影片，讓資料共享。畜產種原資訊網一年服務超過3,000戶畜牧場，並年增50,000筆以上的有教育價值且電子化的遺傳育種資料。兼顧資料轉化為資訊及知識等出版品成本上，可降低宣導成本外，也讓動物育種資料庫實質地影響到畜產事業體之各商業單位生產效率。
