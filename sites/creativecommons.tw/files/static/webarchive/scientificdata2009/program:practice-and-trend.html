The Current Status and Challenges of Survey Research Data Archive Establishment in Taiwan

學術調查研究資料庫建置現況與挑戰

Ly-yun Chang / 張苙雲
　
Center for Survey Research
調查研究專題中心
Academia Sinica
中央研究院

Professor Ly-yun Chang is Research Fellow at Institute of Sociology and Executive Director for the Center for Survey Research, Academia Sinica. She earned the Distinguished Scholar of the Year 2000, National Science Council. She was the principle investigator for two major nation-wide surveys: “Taiwan Education Longitudinal Panel Study” (2001-2008) and “Taiwan Social Change Survey” (2007-2008). Her recent research projects include “Social Inequality and Health Disparities: Causes, Consequences, and Policy Implications” (2006-2008) and “Health Inequality and Educational Achievement: Fundamental Causality and Intergenerational Transmission” (2008-2010).


abstract / 摘要
  • English
  • 中文

The cost of surveys is rising and it has become increasingly difficult to get high response rates. These obstacles to scholars securing the timely availability of high quality data are what the Survey Research Data Archive has been trying to combat since its establishment in 1994 through the acquisition of raw data collected by questionnaire interviews. Scholars that have joined the SRDA can carry out searches and download datasets online for secondary data analysis.

The main SRDA datasets come from academic research projects and statistical surveys conducted by government agencies. All datasets are examined for wild codes and response consistency according to standard procedures prior to release. We have made considerable efforts not only to foresee and forestall potential common data usage problems, but also to provide support whenever members have difficulties. Extensive value-added strategies are ongoing to enhance data quality and user friendliness. Workshops and promotional activities have also been held to encourage scholars to use a greater variety of secondary data for more in-depth research.

The SRDA is neither a funding agency nor a survey implementing unit. So we have to obtain authorization for public use from copyright owners for each project individually. Based on our own experiences, there is a greater need for awareness in Taiwan of the importance of data sharing. Unfortunately, it seems government agencies are placing stricter limits on the information can be included in datasets. How to secure the confidentiality of data while maximizing its utilization is another important challenge that must be tackled in the near future.

有鑑於執行難度和調查成本日益升高，以及學者對於兼具品質及內涵的調查資料的即時性需求，學術調查研究資料庫 (Survey Research Data Archive, SRDA) 自1994年起開始蒐集台灣地區使用問卷調查得到的原始數據資料，建置網站供國內外學術界人士加入會員後，線上查詢及下載資料再次利用分析。

SRDA 主要以學術性研究及政府機關的調查統計資料為主，為了滿足使用者需求，除了釋出資料前檢核資料正確性並加以格式化之外，我們會針對使用者經常遭遇的問題予以處理，盡力消除使用資料的障礙，並且研擬各種增加資料價值的方案，透過訓練及推廣活動，促進這些調查資料的使用以及增加應用層面的深度和廣度。

由於 SRDA 並非研究委託單位亦非資料收集單位，調查資料需要取得所有權者的授權才能公開讓會員使用，依據以往與學者及研究委託單位接觸的經驗，台灣資料共享風氣仍有提升的空間。此外，政府機關對於能夠開放的調查資料內容限制亦趨嚴格，能否透過公部門與學術界建立信任與合作的關係，依據資料性質設計不同的使用方式來擴大這些調查資料的近用性，亦是一項有待克服的挑戰。

Knowledge Sharing in the Sciences

科學界的知識分享

Kaitlin Thaney
　
Science Commons
Creative Commons　

Kaitlin Thaney is the Program Manager for Science Commons, a project of Creative Commons. Prior to joining the Creative Commons team in 2006, she was the Communications Coordinator for MIT iCampus, a research alliance between the university and Microsoft, centered on education technology. She also spent time working at Reporters Committee for Freedom of the Press, as well as at The Boston Globe newspaper, writing for the City/Region section.


abstract / 摘要
  • English
  • 中文

With more and more content moving to a digital form, the traditional method of scientific publishing and knowledge sharing is changing. Web technology has brought tremendous efficiency gains to commerce, to entertainment, to culture - but the potential has not yet been seized for scientific research. The first step to that lies in making the scholarly content legally and technically available, both the literature and the research data. This talk will explain why access to content is vital to achieving the "Research Web", explore the various tools and policy Science Commons provides to enable this, and look at the growing trend towards opennness.

愈來愈多的內容，漸漸的轉變成數位化形式。傳統的科學出版與知識分享的方法也逐漸改變中。當全球資訊網的技術發展對商業、娛樂、以及文化已產生了巨大影響的同時，在科學研究領域裡，卻還未能充分發揮其應有的潛力。而其中首要的工作，當在於讓學術內容，無論是研究文獻或研究資料，能在法律上與技術上皆可被取用。此次的演講內容，將說明為何內容的取用是達成 Research Web （研究資訊網）的關鍵，同時也將探討 Science Commons 為達成此目標，所提供的各式工具與策略，並提出對 openness （開放）此一趨勢的觀察。

Adopting and Utilizing Creative Commons to Facilitate the Dissemination of Rice Knowledge and Technology

採用與運用 CC 以促進稻作知識與技術的傳遞

Gene Hettel
　
　
International Rice Research Institute (IRRI)
國際稻作研究所

For 36 years, Gene P. Hettel has been an agricultural writer, editor, photographer, and communications specialist in the United States, Mexico, and the Philippines. He received his BS in agriculture and natural resources (1973) and MA in journalism (1980) from The Ohio State University. He is currently editor and head in the Communication and Publications Services unit at the International Rice Research Institute (IRRI), based in the Philippines.


abstract / 摘要
  • English
  • 中文

IRRI’s mission is to reduce poverty and hunger, improve the health of rice farmers and consumers, and ensure that rice production is environmentally sustainable. The Institute works closely with most rice-producing and -consuming countries and their national agricultural research and extension systems as well as farming communities and a range of international, regional, and local organizations.

From a huge volume of research results on rice and rice-related subject matter, IRRI generates and disseminates knowledge and technology as public goods to farmers via its partners in the developing and developed world. In rice research, there is a strong push to provide free and open access to vital communication products in a variety of formats that are convenient to scientists and extension workers located in both the developing and developed world.

During IRRI’s first 46 years, all publications, databases, and software were protected by copyright in accordance with normal publishing practice, i.e., all rights reserved. Then in September 2006, the Institute’s Board of Trustees issued a statement announcing the adoption of a new copyright policy that “IRRI will release its information products (software, documents, multimedia, data), as much as possible, under a suitable open content license.” Such a license will allow copying, distribution, and (usually) the creation of derivative products, prohibit commercialization, and require attribution as well as the release of derivative products under the same license as the original product was released by IRRI, hence, some rights reserved.

Positive aspects of IRRI’s new Creative Commons (CC) licensing policy have led to enhanced co-publication efforts with like-minded institutions, such as the International Plant Nutrition Institute (IPNI). Also, IRRI now has most of its books (both current and historic) on Google Book Search (GBS). IRRI is one of the few publishers to allow full-text viewing on GBS and is perhaps the very first to provide download access to most of its publications via GBS. IRRI currently has a collection of nearly 4,500 digital images in its own Photo Bank and on the public online photo management facility, Flickr. These images are downloadable and free as spelled out in a link to a CC license statement.

The impact of all this is that anyone may now obtain, repurpose, and distribute IRRI publications and photos — and even modify the content for additional enhancement — with proper attribution using CC licenses.

國際稻作研究所 (International Rice Research Institute; IRRI) 的主要任務在於：降低貧窮與飢餓、改善稻米耕作者與消費者的健康、以及確保稻米生產環境的永續性。本研究所積極與稻作生產國、消費國以及相關的農業研究機構、農作組織、以及相關的國際性、區域性、和當地團體密切合作。

IRRI 將過去在稻作以及相關議題的大量研究成果所產出的知識與技術，視為公眾財，並藉由在開發中國家以及已開發國家中所建立的夥伴關係，將成果傳遞給稻米耕作者。在稻作研究領域中，目前有強烈的呼聲與作為，要對不論是開發中國家或是已開發國家的科學家以及相關工作者，提供自由、開放、方便使用的各種格式的重要通訊產品。

在 IRRI 最初的 46 年歷史，所有的出版物、資料庫以及軟體均依照一般的出版實務施行著作權保護；亦即「所有權利保留」(all rights reserved)。不過自 2006年 9月起，IRRI 董事會發佈了一項聲明，宣布 IRRI 將採行新的著作權政策，亦即「IRRI 將盡可能採用開放內容的授權方式，釋出其資訊產品（軟體、文件、多媒體與資料）」。此種授權方式允許對 IRRI 的產品進行複製、散布、以及（通常允許）產出衍生產品，但禁止商業性使用，且要求表彰 IRRI 的機構名稱，並需在採用與原產品相同授權方式之下，始得釋出衍生產品；亦即「部份權利保留」(some rights reserved)。

IRRI 使用創用CC授權這新的政策，帶來幾項好處。首先，與相同觀點的機構之間，強化了彼此共同出版的作為，如與「國際植物營養研究所」(International Plant Nutrition Institute; IPNI) 的合作。同時，IRRI （目前和過去）出版的大部分書籍，也可藉由 Google Book Search (GBS) 服務找到。IRRI 的大多數出版品，是 GBS 服務中少數可被全文瀏覽的，而且也可能是首先能被全文下載的。目前 IRRI 有將近 4,500 張數位影像存於 IRRI 的影像資料庫，並且經由線上影像管理服務如 Flickr 提供給大眾。這些影像可被下載，並以其所連結的創用CC授權條款所明示的方式，被自由使用。

此政策的影響是，現在每個人都可能可以取得、再次使用、以及散布 IRRI 的出版品與影像，甚至允許對釋出內容進行修改以達到另外的效用，只需要依照創用 CC 的授權方式，表彰 IRRI 的機構名稱就可以了。
